## 📌 Project Overview

An end-to-end sales analytics project built entirely in **Microsoft Excel** — featuring an interactive dashboard, pivot tables, 12 dynamic charts, and functional slicers across 3 years of Porsche luxury vehicle sales data across India.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| 🚗 Total Units Sold | 2,520 |

## 🔍 Interactive Filters (Slicers)

> All 12 charts update simultaneously with one click.

**Available Slicers:**
- 🚗 **Model** — 911 Carrera, Cayenne, Macan, Panamera, Taycan
- 🌍 **Region** — Bangalore, Chennai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Mumbai
- 📦 **Units Sold** — Filter by quantity tier (1 / 2 / 3 / 4)
- 📅 **Year** — 2023, 2024, 2025

**How to use:**
1. Open the file in **Microsoft Excel** (not Google Sheets)
2. Navigate to the 🏎️ **Dashboard** tab
3. Click any value in the right-side slicer panel
4. Hold `Ctrl` to select multiple values simultaneously
5. Click the `✕` clear icon on any slicer to reset that filter

> ⚠️ **Note:** Slicers require Microsoft Excel desktop app. Google Sheets does not support slicers or these pivot chart types.

## 📈 Key Insights

- 🏆 **911 Carrera** leads in total revenue at ₹8,852 Cr — contributing **24%** of total revenue
- 🌍 **Mumbai** is the top-profit region despite mid-range sales volume
- 📉 **Higher discount tiers** show diminishing profit returns — discounting strategy needs review
- 🚗 **Macan** drives the highest unit volume with competitive mid-range pricing
- 💎 **Taycan** commands the premium price point but operates at lower volume — ultra-luxury positioning
- 📅 **Q4 months (Oct–Dec)** consistently show the strongest sales performance — clear seasonal pattern
- 💡 **GST contribution** is highest from 911 Carrera, proportional to its revenue dominance
